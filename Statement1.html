STATEMENT

When we were given the theme "unseen" the first thing that came to my mind was invisible, something no one can see just like our thoughts. Through this assignment I tried to represent a part of my thought process.

Our incapability to see the thoughts of one another compels us to communicate to understand what is going on in our minds. We cannot read minds, so we ask, "What is on your mind?" On the second page "SIT", when you proceed into the thought bubble for answer which seems to be blank, leads to "MIND". That page is a representation of my mind. A busy mind, a mind that wonders a lot. And it gets hard at times to be present. I relate my thoughts with bubbles because when you try to touch a bubble, it disappears, just like my thoughts. When ever I try to engage with my thoughts, I jump to another one and most of the time can not remember what I was even thinking. When I am asked what is on my mind it’s hard to explain so I have put a tear drop on that page that leads to negative self talk. Most of the harm that is caused in our head, is by us and our thoughts. On "THOUGHTS" I tried to show the self-critical version of my mind. I can be thinking everything and nothing at the same time, which is often overwhelming. A piece of advice once offered to me was; if I want to feed my problem and let it grow, I should continue thinking about it. However, if I want to starve it, I need to take action. Going for a "WALK" is the first action that I take to get away from my own overwhelming thoughts. Thoughts are unseen but can be disturbing at times. I go for a walk for fresh air and try to remind myself that it’s okay to feel that way. I have placed on this page a heart on one of the buildings. It symbolizes my happy place, within exist the people in my life that are patient with me and cherish our time together. These are the people that are present with me when we attempt to understand the unseen.
